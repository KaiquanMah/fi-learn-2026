# Comprehensive Finnish Language Curriculum (Level 0 – A2.2)

**Target Audience:** English speakers new to the Finnish language.
**Goal:** Progress from zero knowledge to managing familiar topics, simple writing, and ordinary service situations.

---

## **Level 0: The Absolute Basics**
*Goal: Familiarization with sounds, alphabet, and immediate survival phrases.*

### **Topic 1: The Finnish Alphabet & Pronunciation**
*Concept: Finnish is phonetic. Each letter corresponds to exactly one sound. The first syllable is always stressed.*

**10 Key Pronunciation Concepts & Drills:**
1.  **A vs. Ä:** *A* is like "c**a**r". *Ä* is like "c**a**t".
    *   *Drill:* **Sata** (hundred) vs. **Säätä** (weather - partitive).
2.  **O vs. Ö:** *O* is like "h**o**t". *Ö* is like "w**o**rd" (without the r).
    *   *Drill:* **Loma** (vacation) vs. **Hölmö** (silly).
3.  **U vs. Y:** *U* is like "b**oo**t". *Y* is like the French 'u' or German 'ü'.
    *   *Drill:* **Hullu** (crazy) vs. **Hylly** (shelf).
4.  **Double Vowels:** Hold the sound twice as long. This changes the meaning.
    *   *Drill:* **Tuli** (fire) vs. **Tuuli** (wind).
5.  **Double Consonants:** Pause slightly on the consonant.
    *   *Drill:* **Kuka** (who) vs. **Kukka** (flower).
6.  **J:** Pronounced like the English 'Y' in "Yes".
    *   *Drill:* **Juna** (train), **Joo** (yeah).
7.  **R:** Rolled R (trilled), like in Spanish or Italian.
    *   *Drill:* **Rauha** (peace), **Ravintola** (restaurant).
8.  **H:** Always pronounced, even in the middle of a word.
    *   *Drill:* **Puhua** (to speak), **Lahti** (bay).
9.  **Diphthongs (Yö):** Pronounced as one smooth sound "yuo".
    *   *Drill:* **Työ** (work), **Yö** (night).
10. **No Gender:** There is no "he" or "she". Only **Hän**.
